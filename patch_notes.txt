DICEY DESK DUELS — BALANCE PATCH (July 2026)
=============================================

The reroll-economy patch: infinite loops closed, dead picks reworked, and the
face-mod arms race reined in. Winners: combo builds, Gamblers, and anyone who
always wanted Jackpot to actually pay out.

And the pantry door swings open: the KITCHEN biome arrives, with a dozen new
events, ambush minibosses, poison dice, companions that fight at your side, and
bigger brawls. Mind the mold.

REROLLS & SUSTAIN
-----------------
* Bloodroll — paid rerolls past your free pool now cost HP that DOUBLES with
  each use (1, 2, 4, 8…), resetting at the start of your next turn (was a flat
  1 HP forever). Roll responsibly.
* Tide Wall & Bloodletter — only the first 5 rerolls of that die each turn
  grant their shield / healing. The infinite Bloodroll heal-loop is closed.
* Free Reroll — removed from the game. It was quietly warping every reroll
  budget; Patient and Overcharge fill its slot.
* Patient — now grants +3 rerolls per turn (was +1). Still −15% damage.

DIE FEATURES
------------
* Overcharge — reworked: while this die shows a 6, +0.4 combo multiplier per
  level (was: +1 reroll when it showed a 6).
* Whetstone — its +2 per reroll now lands on the damage sum BEFORE the combo
  multiplier. Churn now scales with your combos.
* Banker — unused rerolls now add this die's pip to the sum before the
  multiplier (was flat damage added after). Restraint pays combo dividends.
* Momentum — reworked: +0.1 combo multiplier per consecutive turn the die is
  left unrerolled (was +1 flat damage). Resets when rerolled — and now also
  resets when a new fight starts (no more riding one Anchored die all run).
* Prism — simplified: a flat +0.2 combo multiplier per level, always on
  (was: +0.2 per die currently showing a 6).
* Kindle — its cap now grows with level: +1.0 combo multiplier per level.
* Jackpot — five-of-a-kind now pays 100 beans per level (was 5). Jackpot!
* Shockwave — 8% of primary damage per charge (was 10%); the Amplified
  enhancer is now 12% (was 15%).
* Bubble: Reinforced — +6 flat damage per bubble hit (was +10).
* Aegis & Vital Surge — combo-multiplier scaling reduced to ×1.5 (was ×2).
* Ricochet — bolts hit for 15% of primary damage per level (was 10%).

FACE MODS (they were VERY good — now they're merely great)
----------------------------------------------------------
* Forge a Die, Load a Die, Engrave a Die — capped at 3 pickups per run each.
* Polish a Die — capped at 2 pickups per run.
* Load a Die — reworked: raises a die's two lowest faces by one.
* Engrave a Die — reworked: raises a die's THREE lowest faces by one.
* Brand a Die — now a treasure premium: no per-run cap, but the gift node is
  the only reliable source. Removed from the Reforge workbench (the Whetstone
  Outcrop event can still brand you a die if fortune smiles).
* Reforge workbench — face-mod stamps are limited to 2 per visit.

RUN-WIDE UPGRADES & WORKSHOP
----------------------------
* Ward — grants 5 shield at the start of each turn (was 2).
* Bulwark Stance — +16 shield per turn (was +8). Still −20% damage.
* Natural Healing — nerfed: each purchase adds +1% to between-fight healing
  (was +5%). Attrition matters again.
* Enemy scaling — enemies gain HP slightly faster as you stack rewards
  (offsets this patch's upgrade buffs; overall difficulty lands where it was).

BUGFIXES
--------
* Bulwark + Wildcard: a wild face no longer corrupts your shield value for the
  rest of the fight. A wild now counts as 1 pip for Bulwark's shield.
* Momentum no longer carries its stacks from fight to fight.
* The damage preview now exactly matches the damage dealt for all reworked
  features (Whetstone, Banker, Momentum, Prism, Overcharge).
* Fixed: the Attack button could vanish in crowded fights on smaller screens —
  a summoned enemy would overflow the board and shove the button off-screen.
  Enemies now tuck into a tidy layout and the button always stays reachable.
* Fixed a fatal edge case: if a killing blow and a lethal counter (Thorns, spikes)
  landed on the same beat, the run could end in a loss even though you'd cleared
  the board — or soft-lock. Now clearing the board always wins (yes, even at 0 HP);
  dropping to 0 with a foe still standing is the only way that ends in a loss.
* Combat can no longer soft-lock: if anything unexpected trips mid-turn, the
  fight now recovers to a playable state instead of freezing with a greyed-out
  Attack button (and logs the cause to the console for us to chase down).
* You can now PASS on rewards — a "Skip (take nothing)" button on the reward
  screen lets you decline an upgrade. Bonus: skipping doesn't bump enemy scaling,
  so taking nothing keeps the next fights a touch easier.
* FOUND IT: the ally soft-lock. If a pet struck an enemy that survived your
  attack, its strike animation crashed mid-turn and the Attack button never
  came back. Pets now lunge properly and the turn always hands back to you.
* Allies past the first two now actually show up — extra pets stack onto the
  flanks instead of fighting invisibly from the void.
* Dead bosses stay dead: a boss finished off by Thorns can no longer
  counter-attack you or call reinforcements from beyond the grave.
* Exploding enemies (looking at you, popcorn) no longer resurrect you — the
  "a death-pop can't kill you" mercy rule was quietly reviving players who were
  already dead from something else that turn.
* The Gremlin skin is now actually earnable. Clearing the miniboss at exactly
  0 HP and pressing on unlocks it, as the hint always claimed — previously the
  between-fights heal kicked in first and made the feat impossible to detect.
* Summons and boss reinforcements now respect the six-enemy board cap.
* Fixed: the Gingerbread Man (the L5 kitchen speed-bump) could roll a boss
  signature such as Lifelink and turn unkillable — immune to all attack damage
  behind a totem while only poison chipped through. He's the gentle intro he was
  meant to be again: quick, dodgy, no boss build-checks.

WELCOME TO THE KITCHEN
----------------------
* BIGGER BATTLES — fights now hold up to SIX enemies at once (was four).
  Summoners, get cooking.
* A FULLY STOCKED PANTRY — the kitchen roster is now the whole bestiary: bugs and
  bites, the mold-and-rot poison crew, fuming peppers, and a drawer of appliances,
  across three escalating miniboss gates and a final-boss SNAP.
* POISON, MADE VISIBLE — a 🧪 counter now shows exactly how many poison stacks are
  ticking on you and on each enemy (it halves every turn).
* NEW DIE FEATURES, the poison line:
  - Toxin — each attack poisons your target by that die's PIPS (× level). Roll high
    to hit hard AND poison hard.
  - Venom — every reroll of the die (first five each turn) spits venom onto a
    RANDOM enemy: spread the rot across the whole board.
  - Blight & Nettle round out the set (combo-scaled poison; poison-thorns).
* ALLIES — companions have arrived. Recruit one and it strikes a random enemy
  every turn and sticks with you for the REST of the run (through fights, rests,
  and bosses alike). They flank your hero and lunge in with their own sound —
  and a recruited foe now wears its true face (the Rogue Paperclip and the
  Pitcher's Pitcherling fight in their own sprites).

KITCHEN EVENTS (twelve new "?" encounters)
------------------------------------------
* The Scary Bread Man & The Pitcher — "Investigate" at your peril: each is an
  ambush miniboss. Win and you'll earn a brand-new skin plus a hero's haul of
  rewards. The Pitcher even leaves a companion behind.
* The Garbage Disposal — feed it a die; it comes back carrying Toxin.
* The Spice Rack — three jars of heat: Ghost Pepper (Glass Cannon), an even
  +15% Seasoned blend with no downside, or Custom Brand — turn every face of a
  die you pick into a pip you choose.
* The Vending Machine — insert 100 beans for a random upgrade; take it, or feed
  the machine again for a fresh roll.
* The Compost Bin — strip a die's feature and plant a fresh one from three
  (or walk away with it bare).
* The Talking Teapot — it has something to say, and 20 beans to share.
* The Rogue Paperclip — hire a defector from the desk to fight at your side.
* Kitchen twists on old friends: the Warm Oven Mitt, a Loose Whisk, the Knife
  Sharpener, and a Fortune Cookie.

NEW SKINS
---------
* Bread Man and Pitcher — earned by besting their namesake Kitchen minibosses.
